TL; DR

Weaving resources from #tidytuesday, twitter, and blog posts, I challenged myself to create this interview presentation to gain insights on my prospective employer while showcasing my analytics abilities.

Formulation

Then I faced the dilemma of what to actually present. I wanted to shine as a candidate, but my previous academic experience was tangential to this industry. I took their instructions to heart and decided to create something that I was truly proud of. I developed a project with three goals in mind:

  1. Learn about my prospective employer.

  2. Cultivate my nascent tidyverse skills.

  3. Invest in new analytic methods and coding techniques that could serve future me.

What I did

I spent many hours at the computer. I paused and replayed David Robinsonâ€™s screencast several times. I researched R functions and analytic methods that were new to me. And thenâ€¦

I learned about my prospective employer through their tweets. I explored their tweets over time, common hashtags, and tweet popularity by likes and retweets. I examined trends in hashtags over time, the distribution of likes and retweets among common hashtags, and a network analysis of what hashtags went together. I also delved into common words (but not hashtags) in the tweets, a network analysis of those words, and a lasso model to analyze word associations with likes and retweets. Lastly, I created two shiny apps to accompany the presentation, one to search tweets and another to explore the network analysis of words and hashtags. Yes, it was a lot!
