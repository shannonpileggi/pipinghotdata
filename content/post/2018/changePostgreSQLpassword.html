--- title: "How to change PostgreSQL password in Windows" author: "Shannon Pileggi" date: 2018-10-24 categories: ["R"] tags: ["PostgreSQL", "password", "Windows"] ---

When you first install PostgreSQL on your computer, you are given a default user (postgres) and you are required to supply a password. Don’t lose this! This post is for users who know their password, and would like to re-set it to a different password.

Why would you want to do this? Well, maybe for security reasons perhaps. But my more mundane reason is that I wanted to demonstrate how to get started with PostgreSQL, but not with my own super secret password! I needed to change it to something more… impersonal. So here is how I did it on Windows 10 Enterprise with PostgreSQL 10.

  1. Open pgAdmin by searching for it in the Windows search box.

  2. Click on Servers and you will be prompted to enter your existing password.

  3. Expand the Servers menu to find the default user of postgres. Right-click on postgres and then click on Properties.

  4. Under the Definition tab, enter your new password. Don’t forget to Save at the bottom of this window.

  5. Open Services by searching for it in the Windows search box.

  6. Re-start PostgreSQL with a right-click.

Done!
